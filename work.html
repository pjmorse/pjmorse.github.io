back home

computers

I started playing with computers when my parents brought home an Apple ][+ in the early eighties. I learned a bunch of games, some very basic BASIC (I managed some I/O and conditionals, but never managed flow control or functions) some spreadsheets (VisiCalc) and word processing - not bad on a system not equipped to display lower case characters in most programs. That got me to college essays.

In college I got a Mac LC II, one of the systems built when Apple was actively trying to get American consumers to buy Windows. Still, it got me to the next most important step: the college VAX, a VMS system allowing basic internet functions like email, finger, and talk. That was enough, a starter drug that led me within a semester to our unix system, Usenet, and eventually, in 1994, the Web. I think that's when I really became a geek. I worked for the Academic Computer Center for three years as a Supervisor, taking a three-hour shift weekly (sometimes more) answering everyone else's questions, which taught me even more about computers, including how people interact with them (and how they can screw them up).

I launched my college home page in January 1995. Between then and graduation, I worked on a number of sites at the college, sometimes for pay, sometimes not. I managed to work that into an internship at Runner's World, which eventually became full-time. Through August 2001, I worked as Online Editor (i.e. internet gnome) for RW. In addition to the main site, my office (there ended up being three of us - another alum is now at nikerunning.com) maintained the High School Runner site, the New Runner website, and the Women's Running site. I kept working with Macs, did more with Windows, and learned multiple tools for website tweaking - Perl, BBEdit, regexps, and more (of course).

In September of 2001, I left RW and started work at Sinauer Associates as "media coordinator" (or something like that.) I do websites, CD-ROMS, systems and network administration, and help desk for both our office and for all users of our software. I've learned more than I expected possible about a lot of academia. You can find more details about my work there on my Sinauer page. Aside from having to learn the tools to run our server, I've had to do more Perl and now PHP.

I only took about two courses in Computer Science in college, because a perceived difficulty with math led me out of that path. Some years later, I decided I needed a more solid background geeking. I have ambitions to an advanced degree, but I started out at the Muhlenberg Evening College, and now I take night classes at Westfield State College, working towards a "second bachelors" or something like it. I've gotten in to databases, networking, more calculus, organization and architecture, systems programming...

Working doesn't always mean computers. You'll find a collection of links to articles I've written about running on my clips page. Since leaving RW, I've also had articles in three New York Road Runners' sites, New England Runner, and Running Times.

There's more past work, which you'll find in my résumé. In all the time I've been doing this, I've had my fingers on a fair number of sites, but on most of them, my fingerprints have since been smudged.
