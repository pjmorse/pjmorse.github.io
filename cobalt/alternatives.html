Alternatives

You always have other options.

The two-tier defense system described here, using block-lists and procmail, has worked well for me. However, there are other techniques and technologies available for use in place of (or in combination with) what I've described here.

Malicious-code scanning

  • MIMEDefang is a sendmail "milter" which moves much of the work of The Sanitizer up to the sendmail level.
  • Anomy Mail Tools is another Sanitizer.
  • Amavis is a mail virus scanner.
  • Steve Bassi wrote packages to install mailscanner (free) and F-Prot (license required, check the site).
  • Joe Quinn detailed his experiences installing Mailscanner and SpamAssassin on a RaQ4. Very well done.

Spam blocking

Whitelists

"Whitelisting" and "greenlisting" are methods of spam filtering which involve, in a nutshell, accepting mail from "known good" senders, and subjecting only unknown senders to intense filtering, perhaps even involving a challenge-and-response system to allow the sender to get themselves on the "known good" list. This is based on the idea that spammers will not bother to respond to the challenges. Nancy McGough, who wrote the excellent procmail quick start, has a good explanation of this approach to spam-blocking, including links to some of the notable arguments against it (which can be summarized by the names sometimes applied to the challenge-and-response system, "Prove that you love me" or "jump through my hoops.")

These systems can be custom-built in procmail, if you're patient enough and ready to do a lot of reading in the archives of the procmail list. SpamAssassin will also do whitelisting, if you configure it properly. There are other pre-built systems which will do this:

  • TMDA - Tagged Mail Delivery Agent. There's a VERY good explanation of how this works and what it does on the home page.
  • CMD - Confirmed Mail Delivery, a package by John Conover.
  • Habeas - A sort of institutionalized white list. They explain it better than I do.

General reading

You'll do better administering your spam and anti-virus defenses if you have a better understanding of what they do, and what you're up against.

  • Paul Graham's "A Plan For Spam" started the "Bayesian filtering" wave of filtering software, basing filters on a statistical analysis of what spam looks like vs. what a particular user's real mail looks like, and learning as it goes. Includes links to many of the responses, expansions, and actual software being developed to use this method to block spam.
  • spamfaq.net is the FAQ for news.admin.net-abuse.email (or NANAE) and contains most of the anti-spam "canon."
  • Philip Jacob questions whether we're going about the spam problem in the right way at all.
